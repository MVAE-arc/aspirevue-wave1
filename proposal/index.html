Overview

The short version: I build out your operating stack over 30 days — Plaud, Obsidian, Google AI Pro, Mac Studio M2 — so it runs as a single system instead of a set of independent tools pulling in different directions. By Day 30, you run the loop yourself. The system is documented in a form you can hand someone without sitting next to them while they read it, and your team can see what you're producing in a format that's actually useful to them.

You bought the Plaud and Google AI Pro the same day we talked. That told me more than anything you said. This proposal reflects that same speed.

How We Work

No clock-in, no ticket queue. You call or text when something needs attention — I respond same-day for anything non-urgent, within two hours when it's time-sensitive. One standing weekly session, 60 to 90 minutes, scheduled on your terms. When a workflow needs to land on your machine and talking through it would burn an hour, I take the wheel remotely and we do it together.

Your team gets access to whatever you want them to see. I fold in whoever you bring in, stay out of whatever stays private — that's your call, not mine. The shared workspace lives in your accounts, under your control.

What this doesn't look like: vendors who charge by the hour and need a purchase order before they'll pick up the phone. I'm in your phonebook. You use me when you need me. That's the shape.

Phase 1  ·  Days 1–10

Stack Foundation and Capture Loop

Day 10: You run a full capture-to-structured-note cycle on your own, with me watching. No prompts from me, no real-time guidance. The Mac Studio M2 is configured to your patterns — not a template, yours. Plaud is in the field workflow. Obsidian has an architecture that makes sense given what AspireVue actually produces. Google AI Pro is wired in.

Step 1.1 — Mac Studio M2 Operating Configuration

Before we touch any AI tools, the hardware needs to be right. Account linkage, environment configuration, file system architecture built around how you actually work. Backup and sync layer that won't surprise you six months from now.

Step 1.2 — Capture Layer

Plaud setup and mobile app integration. Then the protocol: when to record, how to tag it, where it goes after. The first five real-world captures we review together — not because you need hand-holding, but because that's where the captures that break a system live, and catching them early is cheaper than fixing them in Week 3.

Step 1.3 — Synthesis Layer

Vault architecture in Obsidian tuned to AspireVue's content shape — investor prep, client work, internal team communication. The pipeline from Plaud transcript to cleaned, linked note. Then the AI-assisted synthesis pass: what gets summarized, what gets structured, what gets discarded. Before this engagement, you were managing a 14-file Claude context stack with 245KB of voice OS alongside ChatGPT, Notion, Granola, and Whisper, all running parallel with no unified system pulling it together. By Day 10, there's one spine. ChatGPT and Notion don't disappear — they just stop being the center of gravity.

What This Phase Replaces

The gap between “thinking it through on a call” and “that thought existing anywhere useful afterward.” Right now, a meeting ends and most of what was sharp in the room evaporates — either into a voice memo no one processes, or into a note that doesn't connect to anything. This closes that loop.

Phase 2  ·  Days 11–22

Workflow Fluency and Asset Production

Day 22: You're not asking “how do I do this” anymore. You're asking “what should I do next.” The stack is producing things your team can read and use — memos, decision frameworks, briefing docs — built through your thinking, not assembled manually afterward.

Step 2.1 — Repeatable Workflows

On Day 11, we identify your three highest-frequency knowledge-work patterns. Investor update prep. Customer-call synthesis. Internal team brief. Whatever they actually are — we confirm on Day 11, not in advance. For each one: a vault template, a capture protocol, a first production run side-by-side. By the end of this step, those three workflows are not experiments; they're the way you do it.

Step 2.2 — Agentic Surface Integration

This is where the stack goes from “organized notes” to “produces work.” The AI layer gets wired into the synthesis loop without breaking your mental model. You stay the Architect. The surface assists. You feed the system; the system doesn't replace your thinking. The distinction between those two modes is one you'll feel clearly by Day 22 — it's not theoretical.

Step 2.3 — Team Visibility Surface

Shared workspace structure: what's visible to the AspireVue AI team, how they consume what you produce, who gets access to what. One handoff demonstration — you produce something, what you produce reaches the AspireVue AI team cleanly. The system doesn't stay inside your head after this phase.

What This Phase Replaces

Meeting-to-meeting evaporation. You do the thinking, do the work, but by the time you need to brief your team or write it up for a different audience, the sharpest version of it is gone. This phase is about making sure what you produce in Architect mode is legible to everyone else without you having to perform “writing it up” separately.

Phase 3  ·  Days 23–30

Codification and Handoff Readiness

By close of Day 30: The system is documented in a form you own, can extend, and can give to a future team member without needing to re-explain the architecture from scratch. Whether you extend or close clean, the stack is yours.

Step 3.1 — System Documentation

You write it; I edit it. One-page operating doctrine: how the stack works, what each tool does, why it's configured the way it is. Cheat-sheets for the three repeatable workflows from Phase 2. The document is yours because you wrote it — edited by someone who knows what needs to be in it, but not authored by the consultant for the client.

Step 3.2 — Failure-Mode Coverage

Three scenarios we work through explicitly: what happens when the AI surface returns garbage (you recognize it, you correct it, you don't lose the underlying thought); what happens when capture fails (recovery protocol so one bad week doesn't wipe progress); what happens when the team needs to operate the stack without you for a stretch. All three get worked through before Day 30 closes.

Step 3.3 — Renewal Decision Gate

Day 30 retrospective — what landed, what's still partial, what's worth carrying into Month 2 if you extend on the rolling structure described above. Phase 2 Extension Menu items sit on top of that baseline if you want to fire one. We scope and price based on what the first 30 days actually produced, not on projections from before we started.

What This Phase Replaces

The scenario where a consultant exits and the thing they built doesn't survive the month. Whatever you've built by Day 30 runs without me.
